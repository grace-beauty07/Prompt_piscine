>* ## How strong the walls need to be
>* ## Where doors and windows should go
>* ## How sunlight comes in
>* ## How air moves inside
>* ## How everything stays standing
>## That “someone” is an architect.
>## Architecture is both art and science; It’s art because buildings can be beautiful, it’s science because they must not collapse.

# Refined prompt 2: "Summarize the techniques of architecture in seven bullet points."
# Output:
>- ## *Structural design* – Making sure the building stands up and stays up. This includes choosing the right shape, supports, and materials so gravity does not win.
>- ## *Spatial planning* – Deciding how rooms and spaces are arranged so people can move comfortably and logically. No maze-like kitchens, ideally.
>- ## *Proportion and scale* – Designing spaces and elements in the right size relationship to each other and to humans. A door should not feel like a castle gate unless you’re actually building a castle.
>- ## *Material selection* – Choosing materials like concrete, steel, wood, or glass based on strength, cost, climate, and appearance.
>- ## *Light and ventilation design* – Planning how natural light and air enter and move through a building to improve comfort and reduce energy use.
>- ## *Sustainability techniques* – Using energy-efficient systems, insulation, renewable energy, and eco-friendly materials to reduce environmental impact. Because overheating the planet is not ideal. 🌍
>- ## *Aesthetic design* – Shaping the visual style of the building through form, color, texture, and detail so it communicates a certain feeling or identity.
