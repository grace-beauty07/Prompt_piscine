## What is the difference between male and female in plants?
>### Male plants (or male parts) produce **pollen**.
>### Female plants (or female parts) produce **ovules**, which become seeds after fertilization.
>### In flowering plants:
>### The *male part* is called the *stamen*.
>### It has:
>* ### Anther → makes pollen
>* ### Filament → holds the anther up
>### The *female part* is called the *pistil* (or carpel).
>### It has:
>* ### Stigma → catches pollen
>* ### Style → tube pollen travels down
>* ### Ovary → contains ovules (future seeds)
>### Some plants are *monoecious*. That means one plant has both male and female parts. Efficient. Self-sufficient. Overachiever energy.
>### Example: Zea mays (corn)
>### Some plants are *dioecious*. That means male and female flowers grow on separate plants. You need two plants to make seeds. Teamwork.
>### Example: Carica papaya (papaya)
>### So the difference in plants comes down to:
>### Male = makes pollen
>### Female = makes ovules/seeds
