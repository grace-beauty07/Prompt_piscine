# Objective: Break complex questions into smaller steps.

## Prompt: "What is the solution to this equation 6(x - 2) = 26?"
-----
## Restating the problem:
>### We are asked to find the value of x that makes this equation true:
>### 6(x − 2) = 26
>### In other words, what number must x be so that when you subtract 2 from it and multiply the result by 6, you get 26?
-----
## Smaller steps:
>### Step 1: Remove the parentheses.
>### Step 2: Isolate the variable term (6x).
>### Step 3: Solve for x.
>### Step 4: Simplify the fraction.
-----
## Step by step solution:
### Step 1: Remove the parentheses.
>### Right now 6 is multiplying everything inside (x − 2).
>### So we distribute the 6.
>### 6 × x = 6x
>### 6 × (−2) = −12
>### So the equation becomes:
>### 6x − 12 = 26
### Step 2: Isolate the variable term (6x).
>### We want x by itself, but right now we have −12 attached to 6x.
>### To remove −12, we add 12 to both sides.
>### 6x − 12 + 12 = 26 + 12
>### This simplifies to:
>### 6x = 38
### Step 3: Solve for x.
>### Right now x is being multiplied by 6.
>### To undo multiplication, we divide both sides by 6.
>### 6x ÷ 6 = 38 ÷ 6
>### This gives:
>### x = 38/6
### Step 4: Simplify the fraction.
>### 38 ÷ 6 can be reduced by dividing both numbers by 2:
>### 38 ÷ 2 = 19
>### 6 ÷ 2 = 3
>### So the simplified answer is:
>### x = 19/3
>### If you prefer a decimal:
>### 19 ÷ 3 ≈ 6.33
### Final Answer:
>### x = 19/3
-----
## Review and Accuracy:
>### Each step is solved using arithmetic logic and inverse operation and the final answer is accurate.
